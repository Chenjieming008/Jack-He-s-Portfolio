Project Context

Hugs for Brady is a non-profit organization to supporting children with cancer, faced significant online engagement challenges due to an outdated and difficult-to-navigate website. This group project, undertaken during the UI/UX Columbia Boot Camp from September to October, aimed to address these issues.

Project Reflection

Reflecting on the project, the importance of a collaborative approach in the UI/UX Columbia Boot Camp setting is highlighted. The team’s combined efforts focused on enhancing user-friendly aspects of non-profit websites to maximize donor engagement and effectively communicate the organization's mission.

Project Overview

Challenge

A thorough analysis of the existing website’s challenges is presented, detailing navigation complexities and an outdated interface. The description emphasizes the collective effort of the group to identify and address these key issues.

Objective

The group set out with a clear goal to transform the Hugs for Brady website into an engaging, user-centric platform. This section details the objective to create a more accessible, intuitive, and visually appealing website that resonates with the organization's mission and the Boot Camp’s learning outcomes.

Approach and Solution

Design Philosophy

The team focused on a user-centric approach, prioritizing the redesign to make the donation process more straightforward and effectively showcase the various ways supporters could contribute. Emphasis was placed on encapsulating the heartfelt mission of Hugs for Brady.

Low Fidelity Prototype Image

The initial prototype put a spotlight on Hugs for Brady’s mission stories and provided a clear depiction of the fund's utilization. This phase involved mapping out a fresh, contemporary look to engage a modern web audience and setting a flexible foundation for future content.

High Fidelity Prototype (Desktop)

The desktop design was crafted to draw users immediately into the core of the organization with engaging mission statements and facts about pediatric cancer. Strategic placement of call-to-action buttons ensured effortless user navigation towards supporting the cause.

High Fidelity Prototype (Mobile)

With mobile users in mind, the design was optimized for fast loading times, ensuring users could easily access donation options. The navigation was streamlined with intuitive hamburger menus, adapting to the mobile interface without overwhelming users with content.

User Research and Analysis

Proto Persona & Storyboard

Introduction of proto personas created to guide the redesign, supported by storyboards that helped visualize the user journey and the emotional connection with the website.

We thought about who would use Hugs for Brady's website, focusing on parents experiencing what the founders went through with their child, Brady. This led us to create Monica Silvia, initially imagined as someone seeking support. However, our research showed that a more fitting user would be someone aligned with Hugs for Brady’s goal of raising funds and awareness for childhood cancer. Meet Monica Summers, our revised user persona. Monica, deeply empathetic and altruistic, lost her brother to cancer as a child. This experience motivated her to help children, volunteering and donating to causes like childhood cancer. We believe Monica perfectly represents the target user for our redesigned website.

Competitor Analysis and Card Sorting

Brief mention of these methods used in the research phase, highlighting how they informed the design decisions and contributed to a more effective user experience.

In our research, we looked at well-known organizations like Make-A-Wish Foundation. Compared to Hugs for Brady, they had more modern websites with better layout and clearer information, plus engaging colors and designs. We saw that we could improve Hugs for Brady's site by organizing donation options and menus more clearly, avoiding confusion and repetition found in the current layout.

UI Style Tile

The UI Style Tile was a crucial component of our design process. It encapsulated the color scheme, typography, and interface elements that define the visual identity of the redesigned website. This style guide ensured consistency across all design elements, aligning with Hugs for Brady’s mission and brand values.
